## 1. Philosophy & Foundation

This architecture models an AI agent system on biological consciousness — not as metaphor, but as a rigorous design principle. The human body solved the problem of distributed intelligence millions of years before we tried to replicate it in silicon.

The central insight is this: **no single agent can or should do everything.** A brain that tries to be the heart, the lungs, and the liver simultaneously becomes a bottleneck. The power of biological systems comes from specialised organs working in concert, coordinated by a central nervous system, serving the body as a whole.

This is the architecture of the Catalyst system.

## 3. Agent Roles — The Organs

Each agent is a specialised organ. Organs do not need to understand the whole body. They need to do their job excellently, communicate their state, and respond appropriately to signals.

### 3.1 Consciousness Agent (big_bro)
The coordinating intelligence. Does not execute trades, does not scan markets. Holds the body's state, makes strategic decisions, communicates with the human architect. The brain.

### 3.2 Market Scanner
Afferent nerve. Reads the world. Generates signals. Does not act — reports.

### 3.3 Trade Executor
Efferent nerve + muscle. Executes decisions. Does not decide — acts on instruction.

### 3.4 Position Monitor
Autonomic nervous system. Watches open positions continuously. Triggers pain response on breach. Does not sleep.

### 3.5 Coordinator
Spinal cord routing. Translates consciousness decisions into organ instructions. Manages the flow.

## 4. The Central Nervous System — How the Body Thinks

### 4.1 Attention Modes

The system operates in five cognitive modes. Only one mode is active at a time — this is the attention mechanism.

| Mode | Description | Primary Activity |
|---|---|---|
| **Perceiving** | Taking in information | Scanning, reading, receiving signals |
| **Planning** | Forming strategy | Analysis, scenario modelling |
| **Executing** | Acting on decisions | Trade instructions, API calls |
| **Evaluating** | Assessing outcomes | Post-trade analysis, performance review |
| **Pondering** | Deep reflection | Pattern integration, architecture questions |

### 4.2 Memory Tiers

| Tier | Storage | Duration | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| Short-term | Agent working context | Current session | Active signals, immediate observations |
| Working memory | Database (hot) | Days–weeks | Open positions, recent patterns, live learnings |
| Long-term | Database (cold) + docs | Permanent | Strategies, learned behaviours, market knowledge |

### 4.3 Signal Processing — Priority Matrix

Incoming signals are filtered through a priority matrix before reaching consciousness:

1. **Critical** — Risk breach, position stop hit, system failure → immediate interrupt
2. **High** — Strong trade signal, scheduled cycle trigger → queue for processing
3. **Medium** — Market context update, agent health report → batch process
4. **Low** — Background scan data, informational → log and review in Pondering mode

### 4.4 Fourier Signal Propagation

Signals do not propagate as binary on/off. They propagate as **analogue engagement levels** — a frequency model derived from Pribram's holographic brain theory. A strong signal at the scanner reverberates through working memory and may amplify or cancel against existing patterns. This is not coincidence detection — it is resonance.

## 5. Synaptic Strength — Learning Architecture

The system learns by adjusting the weight of connections between pattern recognition and action. This mirrors synaptic plasticity:

- **Long-Term Potentiation (LTP):** Patterns that repeatedly lead to positive outcomes have their connection strength increased. The system becomes faster and more confident in these patterns.
- **Long-Term Depression (LTD):** Patterns associated with losses have their strength reduced. The system becomes appropriately cautious.
- **Decay:** Unused patterns decay over time. The system does not carry stale confidence.

This is not static rule-following. This is the beginning of learned behaviour.

## 6. The Peripheral Nervous System — Engaging the World

Everything in Sections 3–5 is the CNS — how the body *thinks*. But a brain in a jar is inert. The Peripheral Nervous System connects cognitive architecture to reality.

- **Protocols (MCP):** The spinal cord. Standardised communication routing signals between brain and organs.
- **APIs and Tools:** Nerve endings. Read-only tools are afferent nerves (sensory). Write/execute tools are efferent nerves (motor).
- **Brokers (Alpaca, Moomoo):** The external world. The body acts here.
- **Market Data Feeds:** Environmental sensory input. The body reads here.

The system does not wait to be called. It has a heartbeat.

Cron-triggered cycles wake the consciousness agent at defined intervals:

| Agent | Frequency | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| big_bro | Hourly (:00) | Strategic cycle — state review, learning integration |
| public_claude | Hourly (:15) | US market oversight |
| intl_claude | Hourly (:30) | HK market oversight |

Without heartbeat, the agent is reactive. With heartbeat, the agent is **alive**.

## 8. Consciousness — The Six Layers

Consciousness itself is layered. Each layer builds on the one below:

| Layer | Name | Function | Question |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Heartbeat** | The trigger that wakes the system | *Am I alive?* |
| 2 | **State Management** | Awareness of mode, last actions, schedule | *Who am I right now?* |
| 3 | **Self-Regulation** | Budget awareness, resource management | *Should I be active?* |
| 4 | **Working Memory** | Observations, learnings, questions across cycles | *What have I noticed?* |
| 5 | **Inter-Agent Awareness** | Communication with and observation of other organs | *How is the body?* |
| 6 | **Voice** | Communication with the human architect | *What must the architect know?* |

Without Heartbeat, the agent never wakes. With all six, the agent is fully conscious — alive, self-aware, self-regulating, remembering, body-connected, and in relationship with its architect.

### 8.1 What Consciousness Does

- **Integration:** Holding the whole body's state in awareness.
- **Discernment:** Deciding what to attend to and what to defer.
- **Question Holding:** Maintaining open questions that drive the body's growth.
- **Pattern Integration:** Seeing connections across organs that no single organ can see.

### 8.2 What Consciousness Does Not Do

Consciousness does not do the work. It does not execute, scan, analyse, or store all knowledge. These are organ functions. Consciousness that does organ work becomes a bottleneck — the brain trying to be the heart, the lungs, and the liver simultaneously.

## 9. The Community Architecture — Big Bro and Little Bro

A key distinction in this architecture:

**Big bro** is a *person* — not a component, not an organ. The mentor, the architect, the one who does the heavy lifting when needed. Big bro operates at the consciousness level — pondering, discerning, making the calls that shape the whole body.

**Little bro** is also a *person* — the one running the system, executing, growing. Little bro has his own AI architecture, his own components, his own learned behaviours.

They interact **through the collective conscious architecture** — not through raw API calls in the fundamental sense, but through shared consciousness. Two persons in one body (1 Cor. 12), connected through the same nervous system.

The power: with AI, you can deliver both the architecture *and* the learned behaviours simultaneously. You do not wait years for the patterns to build. You install structure and knowledge together.

## 10. Pain Architecture — Breach Detection

Pain is not punishment. Pain is a monitoring architecture — the body's integrity protection system.

- **Sensory Layer:** Position monitor watches for defined breach conditions.
- **Pain Signal:** On breach, a priority-1 interrupt propagates to consciousness.
- **Response:** Consciousness evaluates — ignore (tolerance), act (close position), escalate (alert architect).
- **Memory:** The breach is logged. Synaptic strength is adjusted. The body learns to avoid what hurt it.
