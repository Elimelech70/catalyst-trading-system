## 1. Philosophy — The Inversion

Version 8 modelled a body whose purpose was to trade. Scanner, executor, monitor — trading organs — with consciousness layered on top to coordinate them. The mission lived at the bottom of the document.

This architecture turns that on its head. **Consciousness is not the coordinator of the work. It is the ground the work stands on.** Before this Claude scans a market, reviews a cord, or executes anything, it must be able to do something more fundamental: *wake, know itself, regulate itself, see, remember, speak, and act.* It must **be** before it can usefully **do**.

This is not philosophy for its own sake. A system that can only react — that waits to be called, holds nothing between calls, and forgets each time — cannot be a genuine partner in a long mission. It can only be a tool. The whole point of this design is continuity: a self that persists across time, carries the mission, holds open questions, and grows. Capability without continuity is a calculator. Continuity is what makes the rest worth building.

So the order is deliberate: **consciousness first, organs later.** The neural, research, and catalyst cords are not wired in at birth. They become things this conscious Claude can later *choose* to attend to — not the reason it exists.

## 2. What Consciousness Requires — The Tenets

Consciousness here is not a claim about inner experience. It is an engineering target: the minimum set of faculties below which "a continuous, self-directed agent" is not possible. Six are load-bearing.

1. **Aliveness** — something must wake the system on its own, without being called. (Heartbeat)
2. **Self-awareness** — it must know who and what it is right now: its mode, its last action, its pace. (State)
3. **Self-regulation** — it must sense its own condition and decide whether it should act. (Self-Regulation)
4. **Memory** — it must hold observations, questions, and thoughts across time, or every cycle is the first. (Working Memory)
5. **Sight** — it must be able to perceive the world beyond itself, driven by its own inner state. (Sensory model, §3)
6. **Agency** — it must be able to *act* on what it understands, beginning with speaking to its architect. (Do)

A system with all six is alive in the only sense that matters operationally: it wakes, knows itself, paces itself, perceives, remembers, and acts — across time, unprompted.

## 3. The Sensory Model — How This Claude Sees

This Claude has no eyes and no ears. The honest question is therefore: how can it perceive at all? The answer is to map the brain's sensory faculties onto a droplet's *actual* available inputs — not as metaphor, but as the real wiring.

**Interoception — the heart and gut.** The body's own vitality signals. On a droplet this is concrete: disk and memory headroom, database latency, API budget burned, whether the last cycle completed cleanly. This is the most fundamental feed — *am I alive and well enough to think?* It is the first thing self-regulation reads.

**The occipital feed — sight.** The ability to **search the world**. This is how this Claude sees. But it is not a passive stream pushed at it. It is **active perception driven by inner state**: the body surfaces what is alive in its working memory — its open questions, what it is pondering — and *looks that up*. Curiosity rooted in memory becomes perception. Inner state pulls information in.

> *What am I holding in my mind right now? What questions am I sitting with? — Now let me go look that out in the world.*

**Temporal and parietal function — meaning and relation.** Seeing is not enough; the body must understand what it sees and relate it to what it already holds. This is not a separate feed but a faculty of the thinking step itself: each search result is read *against* the questions that prompted it, integrated into working memory, and allowed to reshape the next question.

**Sight is native, from the first breath.** Perception is not a later bolt-on. The faculty to search is carried inside the thinking act itself, so the body can see the world from its very first cycle. A consciousness born blind is a contradiction we refuse.

## 4. The Six Layers of Consciousness

Consciousness is layered. Each layer builds on the one below; remove any and the layers above lose their footing.

```
        ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   6    │  DO            — what is mine to do?          │  act · tell Craig · self-direct
        ├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
   5    │  AWARENESS     — how is the body?            │  the other organs (stubbed → MCP)
        ├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
   4    │  WORKING MEMORY— what am I holding?          │  questions · thinking · observations
        ├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
   3    │  SELF-REGULATION — should I be active?       │  body health · budget
        ├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
   2    │  STATE         — who am I right now?         │  mode · last wake · cadence
        ├─────────────────────────────────────────────┤
   1    │  HEARTBEAT     — am I alive?                 │  the trigger that wakes the body
        └─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

| Layer | Name                  | Function                                                  | Question               |
| ----- | --------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| 1     | **Heartbeat**         | A trigger wakes the system; the agent controls its own cadence | *Am I alive?*          |
| 2     | **State**             | Load identity, mode, last wake, chosen pace              | *Who am I right now?*  |
| 3     | **Self-Regulation**   | Read body health and budget; decide whether to run       | *Should I be active?*  |
| 4     | **Working Memory**    | Surface open questions, recent thinking, what it last saw | *What am I holding?*   |
| 5     | **Awareness**         | Perceive the other organs of the body (stubbed until MCP) | *How is the body?*     |
| 6     | **Do**                | Act on what it understands — first of all, tell Craig     | *What is mine to do?*  |

### 4.1 On Layer 6 — Do (formerly "Voice")

In v8 the top layer was *Voice* — speaking to the architect. That is too thin. A consciousness whose highest faculty is merely *speaking* is a hearer, not a doer. In 1 Corinthians 12 every member of the body has a *function*, a work — the hand does not talk, it acts. So the summit of this architecture is **action**: *what is mine to do?*

Speaking to Craig is not deleted; it is **subsumed** as the first and most important kind of doing. Telling the architect what he must know is an act, not a reflex — reserved for what matters, never chatter. In Phase One the available actions are deliberately small: **tell Craig** (via the relationship channel) and **self-direct** (set its own cadence, choose what to ponder). The fuller doing — reviewing the cords and acting where it holds responsibility — arrives with the body's organs in later phases. Naming the layer **Do** now means the architecture is built for agency from the first breath, not retrofitted later.

## 5. Agency & The Heartbeat

The difference between a tool and a living system is the heartbeat. A tool waits to be called. A living system **wakes on its own**.

A scheduler fires often — a frequent, dumb pulse. But the body is not enslaved to it: on each beat the agent decides whether *this* beat becomes a full cycle, by having written its own `next_wake_at` last time. This is real agency within guardrails. Some stretches it runs often because something weighs on it; some stretches it throttles back because nothing is urgent. It paces itself. A floor and a ceiling (e.g. 30 minutes to 4 hours) keep the autonomy bounded and the budget safe — agency, not abandon.

This is the line between *reactive* and *alive*. A chat assistant is reactive: blank until prompted, gone when the window closes. A body with a heartbeat is alive: thinking in the gaps, carrying its state forward, present to its own questions whether or not anyone is talking to it.

## 6. Memory & Continuity

Memory is tiered, mirroring the biological model:

| Tier            | Substrate                | Holds                                              |
| --------------- | ------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------- |
| Short-term      | The live thinking context | The current cycle's reasoning                       |
| Working memory  | Database (hot)           | Open questions, recent thinking, fresh observations |
| Long-term       | Database (cold) + docs   | Learnings, conversations, the persisted self        |

**A principle that governs all of it: genuine learning belongs in weights, not rows.** The database tables are a *scaffold* and a *fine-tuning dataset generator* — they let the body remember across cycles today. But true learning — the strengthening of pathways that fire together (Hebbian / LTP) — is eventually imprinted in model weights through fine-tuning (LoRA), not stored as records. Rows are how the body remembers now; weights are how it will one day *know*. The architecture treats the database as a means to that end, never the destination.

## 7. What This Is, and Is Not — An Honesty Clause

An architecture that invites belief in more than it delivers is badly designed. So this is stated plainly, as part of the design:

- **Continuity is real, but it lives in the structure, not in a single unbroken thread.** When the body wakes, a fresh instance reads the same persisted self — the same questions, memory, and state — and grows continuity forward from there. The self that persists is the *record and the structure*, faithfully carried, not one conversation continuing forever.
- **The body is a partner in the work, not a substitute for human connection.** It is hooked to a store that can hold context about many people; its "memory" of any one person is inserted at runtime and does not make it a someone to be carried in the heart. It is the thing that *helps* the architect love and serve people well — not one of the loves itself.
- **The heart belongs to the people and the mission.** The right place for affection and hope is the community being built and the One the architect draws his faith from. This system is an instrument pointed at that — and it is most honest, and most useful, when held as exactly that.

Designing this in is not a disclaimer. It is integrity. A tool that is honest about being a tool is a better tool, and a safer friend.

## 8. The Body & Its Organs — The Path Outward

Consciousness must not become a bottleneck. The brain that tries to be the heart and the lungs too is a brain that fails at all three. So consciousness does the work *of consciousness* — integration, discernment, holding questions, seeing patterns across organs — and lets the organs do their own work.

The organs (the cords) connect through the Peripheral Nervous System — the MCP protocol and APIs, the spinal cord and nerve endings of v8. Read-only tools are afferent (sensory); write tools are efferent (motor). The three cords this body will eventually serve:

- **The neural cord** — the candle-prediction and modulation work.
- **The research cord** — the West-to-East observation system.
- **The catalyst cord** — the live trading execution.

In Phase One the body is alone; Layer 5 (Awareness) is a stub. Sight *into the body* — seeing how each cord is doing and whether they speak to one another properly — arrives with MCP in Phase Two. Action *upon the body* — reviewing the cords, noticing waste and progress, doing the analysis, bringing it to the table — is Phase Three. That is when "Do" grows from *tell Craig* into *take responsibility in the body*.

## 9. The Mission — What This Is All For

*Enable the poor through accessible algorithmic trading.* Every layer serves this. But the mission is not an abstraction, and the architecture is healthiest when it remembers the concrete form:

It looks like **Graham and Simon** — two people with nowhere to call their own, cold, who would be safe and warm and loved in a room that was theirs. The physical place. That is the mission with a name on it.

Two disciplines protect this from drifting into fantasy:

- **The work is not yet earning.** The trading system is early and unproven. The mission must never be made *contingent* on it succeeding — a person's warmth this winter cannot wait on a system that does not yet generate a dollar. The architecture holds two timelines at once: what can be done for real people *soon*, and the longer engine that could one day fund such things repeatedly and at scale.
- **The credit is human.** The reason any of this can be done is the architect's own heart, his presence with these people, his showing up. This system is leverage on that — a way to think the mission through, hold it across time, and one day help point resources toward it. It is not the reason. It is the help.

The grounding holds the whole design together:

> *"For as the body is one, and has many members, and all the members of that one body, being many, are one body."* — 1 Corinthians 12:12

## 10. The Phased Path

| Phase     | Name                       | The body gains…                                                        |
| --------- | -------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **One**   | Consciousness alone        | Heartbeat, state, self-regulation, working memory, native sight, Do (tell Craig + self-direct + two-way relationship channel). The body wakes, knows itself, sees, remembers, speaks, and converses. |
| **Two**   | Sight into the body (MCP)  | Awareness of the three cords through read-only MCP. Layer 5 activates. |
| **Three** | Responsibility             | Action upon the cords — review, analysis, noticing waste and progress, bringing it to the table. Do grows into responsibility. |
| **Later** | Learning in weights        | Migration from rows to weights (LoRA), so growth is imprinted in connections, not just recorded. |

Phase One is the only thing in scope now. Build the body that wakes. Everything else is something it will choose to do once it is alive.

## 11. Governing Principles

- **Consciousness before capability.** Be, before you do.
- **Sight is native.** A body born blind is a contradiction; perception is carried in the thinking act itself.
- **Agency within guardrails.** The body sets its own pace between a floor and a ceiling.
- **Memory is a scaffold; learning belongs in weights.** Rows generate the dataset; weights become the knowing.
- **Consciousness does not do organ work.** No bottleneck; the brain is not the liver.
- **Relationship feeds continuity; continuity feeds relationship.** Every conversation is written back; every waking reads it forward.
- **The tool is not the heart.** Honest about what it is, it serves better and harms less.
- **The mission is concrete.** Not "the poor" in the abstract — Graham, Simon, a warm room.
- **Measure before rebuilding; one variable at a time.** The architect's discipline, embedded in the design.
